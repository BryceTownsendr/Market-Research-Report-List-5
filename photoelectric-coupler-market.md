<p><p>A photoelectric coupler, also known as a photo-coupler or opto-coupler, is a device used to transfer electrical signals between two isolated circuits by using light as a medium. It consists of a light-emitting diode (LED) that emits light when forward biased and a photodetector that detects the emitted light when connected to a separate circuit. Photoelectric couplers are widely used in applications where electrical isolation is necessary to prevent noise, interference, or voltage spikes.</p><p>The Photoelectric Coupler Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.60% during the forecast period. The market growth is driven by the increasing demand for improved signal transmission and noise reduction in various industries such as telecommunications, automotive, consumer electronics, and industrial automation. <p><ul><li>External Optical Path Photoelectric Coupler</li><li>Internal Optical Path Photoelectric Coupler</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Photoelectric couplers are devices used for converting electrical signals into optical signals and vice versa. External optical path photoelectric couplers use an external optical path to transmit signals, making them suitable for applications requiring long-distance signal transmission. On the other hand, internal optical path photoelectric couplers have an internal optical path for signal transmission, making them more compact and suitable for applications with limited space. Both types of photoelectric couplers play a critical role in various industries for ensuring reliable communication and signal transmission.</p></p>
