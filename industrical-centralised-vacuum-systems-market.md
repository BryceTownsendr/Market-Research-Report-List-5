<p><p>Industrial centralised vacuum systems are divided into two main types: cyclonic central vacuum systems and filtered central vacuum systems. Cyclonic central vacuum systems use centrifugal force to separate debris from the air stream, whereas filtered central vacuum systems use filters to trap debris. </p><p>Cyclonic systems are typically more efficient at removing larger particles, while filtered systems are better at capturing smaller particles. Each type has its own advantages and limitations, and the choice between the two depends on the specific needs and requirements of the industrial setting.</p></p>
