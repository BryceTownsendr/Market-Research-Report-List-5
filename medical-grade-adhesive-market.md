<p><p>Medical grade adhesives are classified into two types - Natural and Synthetic. Natural adhesives are derived from biological sources such as proteins or polysaccharides, offering biocompatibility and low toxicity. On the other hand, synthetic adhesives are chemically engineered compounds that provide strong bonding properties and durability. Both types of adhesives are widely used in the healthcare industry for applications such as wound closure, medical device assembly, and drug delivery systems. The choice between natural and synthetic adhesives depends on specific requirements of the application.</p></p>
