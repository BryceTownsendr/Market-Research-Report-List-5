<p><p>The Stroke Prevention in Atrial Fibrillation (SPAF) Treatment market includes Oral Direct Thrombin Inhibitors and Oral Direct Factor Xa Inhibitors. These medications are commonly used in patients with atrial fibrillation to prevent strokes caused by blood clots forming in the heart. Oral Direct Thrombin Inhibitors work by blocking the action of thrombin, a key enzyme in the blood clotting process. Oral Direct Factor Xa Inhibitors, on the other hand, work by inhibiting Factor Xa, another enzyme involved in blood clot formation. Both types of medications are effective in reducing the risk of stroke in patients with atrial fibrillation.</p></p>
