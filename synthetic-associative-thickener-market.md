<p><p>Synthetic associative thickeners are widely used in various industries for their ability to enhance viscosity in formulations. They can be categorized into two main types based on their ionic charge: anionic and nonionic. Anionic thickeners have negatively charged groups that interact with water molecules, while nonionic thickeners do not have a charge and rely on hydrogen bonding for thickening. Both types offer unique benefits and are chosen based on the specific requirements of the formulation.</p></p>
