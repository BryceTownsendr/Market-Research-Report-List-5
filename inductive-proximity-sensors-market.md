<p><p>Inductive proximity sensors are widely used in industrial automation for detecting the presence of metallic objects without physical contact. There are two main types of inductive proximity sensors - NPN and PNP. NPN sensors require a positive voltage to be applied to the sensor's input, while PNP sensors require a negative voltage. These sensors are popular due to their reliability, durability, and ability to function in harsh environments. They are used in various applications such as machine tooling, packaging machinery, and assembly lines.</p></p>
