<p><p>Whole slide scanners are devices used in pathology to digitize entire tissue slides for better analysis and storage. There are two main types of whole slide scanners: Brightfield and Fluorescence. Brightfield scanners use visible light to generate high-resolution images of stained tissue samples. Fluorescence scanners, on the other hand, use specific wavelengths of light to detect fluorescent signals in samples, allowing for the visualization of specific molecules or cellular structures. Both types play a crucial role in modern pathology and biotechnology research.</p></p>
