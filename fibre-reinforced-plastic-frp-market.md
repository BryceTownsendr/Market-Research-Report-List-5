<p><p>Fibre Reinforced Plastic (FRP) is a composite material consisting of a polymer matrix reinforced with fibers, such as carbon or glass. The market for FRP can be divided into two types based on the type of precursor material used: PAN-Based and Pitch-Based. PAN-Based FRP utilizes fibers derived from polyacrylonitrile, offering high tensile strength and stiffness. Pitch-Based FRP, on the other hand, uses fibers derived from coal tar or petroleum pitch, providing enhanced resistance to chemicals and temperatures. Both types cater to different industrial applications depending on their unique properties.</p></p>
