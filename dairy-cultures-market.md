<p><ul><li>Thermophilic Type</li><li>Mesophilic Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dairy cultures are classified into two main types based on their temperature requirements: thermophilic and mesophilic. Thermophilic cultures thrive at higher temperatures, typically used in the production of yogurt and some types of cheese. Mesophilic cultures, on the other hand, thrive at lower temperatures and are commonly used in the production of soft cheeses and fermented dairy products. Both types play a crucial role in determining the final flavor, texture, and aroma of dairy products in the market.</p></p>
