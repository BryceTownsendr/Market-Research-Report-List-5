<p><p>District heating and cooling systems are centralized systems that provide heating and cooling services to multiple buildings within a specific area. District heating involves using a network of insulated pipes to distribute hot water or steam from a centralized plant to buildings for heating purposes. District cooling, on the other hand, uses a similar network to distribute chilled water or ice water for air conditioning. These systems are cost-effective, energy-efficient, and environmentally friendly alternatives to individual heating and cooling systems for buildings.</p></p>
