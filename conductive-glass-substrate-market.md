<p><p>Conductive glass substrates are commonly categorized into FTO (Fluorine-doped Tin Oxide), ITO (Indium Tin Oxide), and Others. FTO substrates are cost-effective and offer good transparency, making them suitable for solar panels and electronic displays. ITO substrates are highly conductive and transparent, making them ideal for touchscreens and LCDs. The "Others" category includes alternative conductive coatings such as graphene. Each type of conductive glass substrate offers different properties and characteristics, catering to various applications in the electronics and renewable energy industries.</p></p>
